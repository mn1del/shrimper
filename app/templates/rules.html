Rules

1) Handicap scoring (Personal Handicap “PHC”)

What it does

Everyone has a personal handicap expressed in seconds per hour (s/hr). Your finish time is adjusted by this number so boats of different speeds can compete fairly.

How your handicap-adjusted time is calculated

  1. Elapsed time = Finish time − Start time (in seconds).
  2. Elapsed hours = Elapsed time ÷ 3600.
  3. Handicap allowance (seconds) = Your handicap (s/hr) × elapsed hours.
  4. Handicap-adjusted time = Elapsed time − allowance. (A negative handicap reduces your time; a positive handicap increases it.)

How the race is ranked for results

  • Boats are ordered by handicap-adjusted time (lowest is 1st).
  • Ties on adjusted time share the same place; the next place is skipped (e.g., 1, 2, 2, 4…).
  • “Line-honours” (absolute finishing order) is shown for interest only; scoring uses the handicap order.

How your handicap changes after each race

Your finishing place on handicap determines a base change to your handicap:

The actual change is the base change × scaling factor (rounded to the nearest whole second per hour).

Revised handicap = Your previous handicap + actual change. This revised handicap becomes your starting handicap for your next race.

Notes: Start-of-season and new-joiner handicaps are set manually. Only finishers count toward the “fleet size” used for scaling.

What it does

Rewards higher finishing places with more points. Most points wins the League standings.

  • DNF/DNS/DSQ: 0 points.
  • Ties share the same points (competition ranking).

Series/season result

Sum your League points across the races counted for that table. Highest total wins (tie-breaks can be agreed by the Race Committee if needed).

3) Traditional points (Low-points system)

What it does

Classic “low-points” scoring on handicap order. Lowest total wins.

Per-race points

  • If you finish on handicap, your points equal your place (1 for 1st, 2 for 2nd, etc.).
  • If you do not finish (DNF/DNS/DSQ), your points are number of finishers + 1 for that race.
  • Ties on handicap-adjusted time receive the same points; the next place is skipped (1, 2, 2, 4…).

Series/season result

For each series, total your race points to get a series score. Because different series may complete different numbers of races, each series score is adjusted according to the number of races completed in that series.

The season grand total is the sum of these adjusted series scores; the lowest grand total wins (tie-breaks as per Sailing Instructions/Race Committee guidance).
